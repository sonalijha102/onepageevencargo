Introduction

Mobility is not just about movement—it is about access to opportunities, independence, and empowerment. For women across India, mobility challenges often act as a barrier to education, employment, and social participation. Women Mobility Centers (WMCs) are innovative, inclusive spaces designed to bridge this gap by enabling, enhancing, and empowering women's mobility—especially for those from underserved or marginalized backgrounds.

What is a Women's Mobility Center?

A Women Mobility Center is a dedicated facility or ecosystem that supports women in acquiring, enhancing, and utilizing mobility skills. These centers provide training, resources, and employment linkages focused on non-traditional sectors like professional driving, delivery services, transport services, and vehicle maintenance. WMCs serve as skill-building, support, and empowerment hubs that aim to break gender stereotypes in the mobility and logistics sector

Even Cargo’s Model

Counseling and mentorship programs provide women with the necessary emotional and professional guidance to navigate challenges in mobility-based careers. These initiatives offer one-on-one support, career counseling, and skill-building advice to help women build confidence, make informed decisions, and achieve financial independence. By connecting with experienced mentors, women gain valuable insights into overcoming workplace barriers and personal hurdles.

● Mentorship, financial literacy, and hands-on experience

● Support for self-reliant women entrepreneurs in the sector

● Breaking barriers in a traditionally male-dominated industry

Objectives

Skill Development

We aim to enhance skills through workshops, training, and mentorship programs. Our goal is to bridge the gap between education and industry needs by offering hands-on learning experiences, ensuring individuals are well-equipped for the workforce.

Economic Empowerment

Providing financial education and entrepreneurship support to boost economic growth. This includes access to microloans, investment guidance, and financial literacy workshops to help individuals manage their resources effectively and achieve financial stability.

Safety and Support

Creating safe environments and offering support systems for individuals in need. We emphasize mental health awareness, self-defense training, and access to helplines and community support groups to ensure overall well-being and protection.

Leadership Development

Encouraging leadership skills through mentoring and hands-on experience. Our program fosters confidence, public speaking, decision-making, and strategic thinking abilities, preparing individuals for leadership roles in various fields.

Digital Literacy

Equipping individuals with essential digital skills to thrive in the modern workforce. Training includes coding, cybersecurity awareness, and effective use of online tools to enhance productivity and competitiveness in the job market.

Environmental Awareness

Promoting sustainable practices and eco-friendly initiatives in various industries. Our programs focus on waste reduction, renewable energy solutions, and climate change advocacy to create a greener future.

Social Inclusion

Encouraging equal opportunities and fostering inclusive environments for all individuals. We work towards eliminating discrimination, promoting diversity, and ensuring accessibility in workplaces and educational institutions.

Core Components

Driving Training

Women receive hands-on training with professional instructors, helping them gain confidence behind the wheel.

The training covers road navigation, parking, defensive driving, and handling emergency situations to ensure safe and effective mobility.

Road Safety Education

This module covers comprehensive traffic rules, road signs, pedestrian rights, and accident prevention techniques.

Women are educated on how to interact with traffic law enforcement and mitigate risks while traveling on the road.

Vehicle Maintenance

Women are trained in basic vehicle upkeep such as tire changing, oil checking, battery maintenance, and handling minor repairs.

This helps them become self-reliant in managing their vehicles and reduces dependency on mechanics.

Employment Linkages

We connect trained women with top logistics companies, providing sustainable employment and financial independence.

Our initiative collaborates with industry leaders to ensure long-term career growth, financial stability, and job security for women entering the logistics sector.

Our network of corporate partners offers women job placements in last-mile delivery, transportation, and logistics sectors.

We build strategic alliances with businesses to create career opportunities that empower women with stable incomes and professional growth.

We provide career growth opportunities, mentoring, and skill development to help women advance in their professional journeys.

Through mentorship programs and specialized training, we support women in achieving leadership roles and higher-paying positions in the logistics industry.

Support Services

Counseling and Mentorship

Counseling and mentorship programs provide women with the necessary emotional and professional guidance to navigate challenges in mobility-based careers.

These initiatives offer one-on-one support, career counseling, and skill-building advice to help women build confidence, make informed decisions, and achieve financial independence. By connecting with experienced mentors, women gain valuable insights into overcoming workplace barriers and personal hurdles.

Gender Sensitization Workshops

By learning to drive and manage their own transport, women can take on well-paying roles in driving, delivery, and ride-sharing services.

This financial empowerment reduces dependence on family members and promotes economic self-sufficiency. These workshops also educate men and women on gender equality in the transport industry.

Legal and Rights Awareness

Women’s access to mobility-oriented employment helps bridge the gender wage gap.

With skill-building initiatives, they secure stable jobs, gain equal pay opportunities, and contribute more effectively to household and national economies. Women are also educated about their legal rights and protection laws related to workplace safety.

Infrastructure & Resources

Training Vehicles (Two-Wheelers and Three-Wheelers)

Providing women with access to well-maintained two-wheelers and three-wheelers for hands-on training is a crucial aspect of skill-building.

These vehicles help trainees develop confidence and expertise in handling different types of transportation, preparing them for roles in ride-sharing, delivery services, and personal mobility. Practical learning enhances road safety awareness and driving efficiency.

Classrooms

Well-equipped classrooms provide structured learning environments where women receive theoretical lessons on traffic regulations, road safety, and vehicle maintenance.

These spaces foster interactive discussions, guest lectures from industry professionals, and workshops that empower women with the knowledge required for mobility-based careers.

Digital Tools for Learning and Tracking Progress

Digital platforms and mobile applications enable trainees to access e-learning modules, track driving hours, and measure their progress.

With the help of simulators, GPS-based route guidance, and virtual assessments, women can enhance their skills efficiently. These tools also facilitate real-time feedback from trainers, ensuring continuous improvement and engagement.

Target Groups

Women from marginalized or low-income backgrounds

Women from marginalized communities often face financial and social barriers to employment. This initiative provides them with access to skill-based training, economic independence, and a secure work environment, enabling them to break the cycle of poverty.

School and college dropouts seeking alternative livelihoods

Many young women drop out of school or college due to financial constraints or societal pressures. Through specialized training in mobility and logistics, they can gain employable skills and access sustainable career opportunities in the transportation sector.

Women belonging to vulnerable groups

Women from vulnerable communities, including survivors of domestic violence and single mothers, often struggle to find stable jobs. Mobility training and employment in logistics empower them with independence, safety, and a reliable source of income.

Women interested in entrepreneurship in transport or logistics

For women aspiring to start their own businesses in transportation or logistics, this program offers mentorship, financial literacy, and hands-on experience. It enables them to become self-reliant entrepreneurs in a male-dominated industry.
